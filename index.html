Dr. Ohad Elishco

Assistant Professor · Electrical & Computer Engineering

Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, Beer-Sheva, Israel

I am an assistant professor in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Ben-Gurion University of the Negev. My research lies at the intersection of coding theory and dynamical systems, with interests in constrained coding, distributed storage, DNA-based data storage, stochastic processes, and symbolic dynamics. I am always looking for motivated M.Sc. and Ph.D. students and postdocs to join my group.

Core Research Interests

data_object

Coding for Constrained & Distributed Systems

Studying the fundamental limits of information storage and transmission in systems with structural constraints. Research includes semiconstrained systems, symbol-pair read channels, burst-deletion correcting codes, and recoverable distributed storage architectures.

Constrained Codes Error Correction Distributed Storage
biotech

DNA Storage & Biological Information Theory

Designing codes tailored to the unique constraints of DNA-based data storage systems. Topics include repeat-free codes, optimal synthesis reference sequences, composite DNA codes, string reconstruction, and the random access problem in DNA storage.

DNA Storage Repeat-Free Codes Biological Channels
query_stats

Symbolic Dynamics, Stochastic Processes & Ergodic Theory

Investigating the long-term behavior of dynamical systems using information-theoretic tools. Research includes the entropy rate and capacity of Pólya string models, the behavior of k-tuple frequencies in mutation systems, and the capacity of dynamical storage systems.

Symbolic Dynamics Ergodic Theory Pólya Urns
