Research

My research lies at the intersection of coding theory and dynamical systems. I am interested in both the theoretical foundations — capacity, entropy, fundamental limits — and in the design of explicit codes and algorithms for modern storage and communication systems. Current areas of focus include constrained coding, distributed storage, DNA-based data storage, stochastic processes, symbolic dynamics, and ergodic theory.

Research Areas

hub Coding Theory

Constrained & Distributed Coding

Establishing capacity results and constructing efficient codes for channels with structural constraints. Topics include semiconstrained systems, symbol-pair read channels, burst-deletion correcting codes, recoverable distributed storage on graphs and grids, and absorption channels.

DNA Storage & Biological IT

Designing codes for DNA-based storage: repeat-free codes, optimal synthesis references, composite DNA codes, string reconstruction, and the random access problem.
